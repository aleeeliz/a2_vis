General sun protection methods of Australians

1 symbol = ~1000 people

The waffle chart indicates that as children progress through school, their likelihood of using sun protection methods decreases, highlighting the need for increased awareness among them.

For the general population, the isotype chart reveals that sunglasses are the most commonly used form of sun protection, while sun-safe clothing ranks last.

It is recommended to use at least three different protection methods; however, only about HALF of Australians adhere to this guideline. There is a clear opportunity for improvement in promoting comprehensive sun safety practices.





The bar chart shows that women generally use SPF more frequently than men across all age groups.

Both genders exhibit the highest diligence in SPF usage within the 35-44 year age group, with a slight bell curve observed around this peak.

Earlier, the 'Daily UV level across different cities overtime (2023)' charts indicate that UV levels are lower during the autumn and winter months (mid-year). Moreover, Darwin and Perth were cities that had often had the highest average daily UV level while the other cities weren't as high.

Nevertheless, consistent sun protection is vital, regardless of season, location, age or gender.
